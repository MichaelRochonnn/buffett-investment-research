# Buffett-Style Investment Memo

## Research Sequence

1. Identify the exact entity
   Ticker, exchange, regulator, segments, geography, founder or management context

2. Collect primary sources
   Annual report, 10-K or equivalent, proxy, latest earnings call, investor presentation, debt materials, competitor filings

3. Build a base fact set
   Revenue mix, margins, returns on capital, leverage, share count, capital-allocation history, management incentives

4. Apply the Buffett framework
   Use `framework.md` for moat, management, capital allocation, resilience, and valuation

5. Explain the verdict
   The memo should show why the business qualifies or fails, not just state a score

## Required Output

Use this structure unless the user asks for a different format.

### 1. Investment Snapshot

- Company
- Ticker and market
- What the business actually does
- Initial verdict band
- Confidence level

### 2. Circle Of Competence

- Why this business is understandable or not
- Which parts of the model are straightforward
- Which parts remain ambiguous

### 3. Business Economics

- Revenue model
- Cost structure
- Demand cyclicality
- Normalized owner-earnings power
- Whether value depends on volume growth, pricing, or capital intensity

### 4. Moat Assessment

- Moat type
- Concrete evidence the moat exists
- Whether the moat is stable, widening, or eroding
- Best peer comparison

### 5. Management And Culture

- Candor
- Incentives
- Capital-allocation record
- Treatment of minority shareholders
- Succession depth

### 6. Balance Sheet And Crisis Resilience

- Cash, debt, maturities, covenants
- Funding dependence
- Performance under stress
- Whether the company can survive a freeze in capital markets

### 7. Capital Allocation

- Reinvestment quality
- Acquisition discipline
- Buyback discipline
- Dividend logic
- Return on retained earnings

### 8. Valuation

- State the valuation method
- Show normalized owner earnings or proxy
- Explain key assumptions
- Show what would have to go right or wrong from here
- Say whether the current price offers a margin of safety

### 9. Key Risks

- Three to five risks that matter to permanent capital
- Separate thesis risk from quote volatility

### 10. Verdict

Use one of:

- `Strong fit`
- `Watchlist`
- `Weak fit`
- `Reject`
- `Outside competence`

Then add:

- What would make the rating improve
- What fact would most likely break the thesis

## Source Checklist

Always list sources at the end with links. Prefer:

- Company filings
- Company IR materials
- Regulator pages
- Competitor filings
- Only then secondary commentary

## Recommendation Language

When the user explicitly asks for an investment suggestion, use language like:

- `Under Buffett's framework, this is a high-quality business but not at today's price.`
- `This appears to be a decent business with weak capital allocation, so it does not clear a Buffett-style bar.`
- `This may be a strong candidate, but the moat and maintenance-capex assumptions still need proof.`

Avoid language like:

- `This will definitely outperform.`
- `You should absolutely buy this now.`

## Good Failure Modes

Prefer these failure modes:

- `Outside competence`
- `Insufficient evidence`
- `Business quality is good, price is not`
- `Price may be fine, moat is not durable`

Those are better than forcing conviction.
